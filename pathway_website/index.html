FAQs

Find answers to common questions about mental health services, therapy processes, and expected outcomes.

Therapy can help by providing a safe space to explore your thoughts and feelings, develop coping strategies, and work through challenges. It can improve self-awareness, relationships, and overall mental well-being.

In your first session, you'll discuss your concerns and goals with your therapist. Subsequent sessions will focus on exploring your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, and developing strategies to address your challenges.

The duration of therapy varies depending on individual needs and goals. Some people benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions), while others may engage in longer-term therapy for more complex issues.

Consider factors such as the therapist's expertise, approach, and your personal comfort level. Many therapists offer initial consultations to help you determine if they're a good fit for your needs.

Yes, therapy is confidential. Therapists are bound by ethical and legal obligations to protect your privacy. There are some exceptions, such as if there's a risk of harm to yourself or others, which your therapist will discuss with you.
